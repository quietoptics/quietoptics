Trust the numbers

Why you can trust the numbers.

Four concrete promises we hold across every app. Here they are, so you can hold us to them.

Principle 01
You never see the answer before you commit.

You make the call before you can see the answer. Whatever you wrote gets sealed and timestamped before the outcome exists, and the reveal only goes one way. You can't peek, then quietly throw out a bad run.

An honest test is one you can't talk your way into passing after the fact. Everything else here depends on this one.

Principle 02
We show you "not enough yet," not a flattering guess.

Every score is measured against plain luck, so a hit has to earn its keep. Nothing shows until there's enough to mean something. Until then you see how much more you'd need, never a noisy chart dressed up as insight.

A flat result is the instrument working, not you failing. We would rather show you an honest "not enough yet" than a flattering number you can't trust. That honesty is the product.

Principle 03
Your journal stays on your phone.

Every app runs entirely on your iPhone or iPad. No accounts, no analytics, no servers. What you write stays yours because there is nowhere else for it to go. Where an app offers optional paid sync, it goes through your own private iCloud and nowhere else.

Principle 04
AI only grades what you already wrote.

Some journals in this studio let a little on-device AI help score an entry. It reads a call you already sealed and checks it against an outcome that already exists.

It can lower a generous score. It can never inflate one, and it never tells you what happens next. One app, Reticle, uses no AI at all, on purpose: your results are your data, not a model's guess about your data.

The stance

We take no position on whether remote viewing, forecasting edges, intention, or precognition are real. Neither does the app. That's exactly why it's worth measuring, carefully, for you. We don't sneer and we don't sell belief. We keep the journal honest and hand you the results.

Maybe it's noise. Maybe it isn't. This is how you'd actually find out.
